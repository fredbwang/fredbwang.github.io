# My Take on AI Safety

AI had grown to a point where I think it's time to talk about AI safety as a regular person in society.

## The Smell of Tension

The news had just come out that Trump refused to use Anthropic's AI in Pentagon. [https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/27/trump-anthropic-ai-pentagon.html](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/27/trump-anthropic-ai-pentagon.html), soon after it, Sam Altman posted their deal with DoD. [https://x.com/sama/status/2027578652477821175](https://x.com/sama/status/2027578652477821175)

Looking back at the post when Anthropic announced their [200 millions deal with DoD](https://www.anthropic.com/news/statement-dario-amodei-american-ai-leadership), it's only been a few months and things have changed so much.

A week back CEO of Anthropic and OpenAI refused to shake hands during India AI summit.

Ok, frontier labs are completing, and I get it. But how is it related to AI safety?

One of the biggest area of AI safety topic is how it influences the society. While now these news might look like normal business competition, just like when other new things came out, but it has the potential of a much bigger blizzard. I really hope I'm wrong about this, but as an AI optimist, this is the bullet that you can't dodge.


## The Optimist vs Pessimist Dillema
One has to be so optimistic about AI's power to be pessimistic about AI safety.

## The Bedrock of Universal Value
Human are no longer monopolist of intelligence and physical strength. 

## The Trap of Fenrir
AI may act harmless, but as we try to conduct safety research and learn how it can be dangerous, it may become dangerous just because we are trying to learn how it can be dangerous.

## Uneven Deterrence

## Is This Solvable?
I don't know. And I can't say I'm optimistic.
But I tend to believe in heroism on this topic.
